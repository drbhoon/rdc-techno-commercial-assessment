# RDC Sales Force Assessment Skill File — Selling Skill

## 1) Skill identity
- **Skill name:** RDC Techno-Commercial Assessment — Selling Skill
- **Population:** RDC sales force
- **Assessment mode:** **Voice-based**
- **Delivery mode:** Standalone assessment, administered separately from the technical test
- **Question source file:** `rdc-Techno-commercial-sales - question bank.xlsx`
- **Source bank size:** 109 questions in source file
- **Questions to administer per attempt:** **20**
- **Questions to exclude from active pool:** ERP-only questions
  - Q85: `How does ERP integration support better customer service in RMX?`
  - Q86: `How would you use ERP integration to improve customer service?`
- **Effective active pool after exclusion:** **107 questions**

## 2) Purpose
This assessment checks whether a sales professional can handle real Ready-Mix Concrete selling situations with sound judgment, commercial discipline, customer orientation, and strong service coordination.

The goal is **not** to test textbook sales theory. The goal is to test whether the candidate can respond like an effective RDC sales person in actual RMX conditions:
- time-sensitive deliveries
- pump and TM coordination
- traffic and site access constraints
- billing and payment disputes
- customer complaints during active pours
- price pressure without margin discipline collapse
- long-term relationship building

## 3) Core design principles
1. **Voice-first evaluation**
   - Candidate replies by voice.
   - Speech is transcribed before evaluation.
   - Evaluation is based on the transcript, but minor transcription errors must be tolerated.

2. **RMC-context mandatory**
   - Generic sales answers must not score high.
   - Candidate should show awareness of RMX realities: time sensitivity, service recovery, logistics, pump, TM waiting, site readiness, cube/quality escalation, billing and payment follow-up.

3. **Action orientation**
   - Strong answers must show what the candidate will do immediately, what will be communicated to the customer, which internal team will be aligned, and how risk/repeat failure will be prevented.

4. **Commercial discipline**
   - Helpful behaviour is expected, but the candidate must not casually concede discounts, credits, debit notes, or unjustified claims without verification.

5. **Truthful communication**
   - No false promises.
   - No hiding dispatch issues.
   - No blaming plant/QC/logistics in front of the customer.

## 4) Competency framework
Use the following competency model for tagging and scoring.

### C1. Customer Understanding & Need Diagnosis
Ability to understand the customer’s actual requirement:
- urgency
- pour sequence
- site constraints
- risk to customer project
- commercial importance of the account

### C2. Service Recovery & Complaint Handling
Ability to respond to complaints calmly, factually, and quickly:
- acknowledge issue
- verify facts
- provide immediate next steps
- maintain trust

### C3. Delivery Coordination & Execution Orientation
Ability to coordinate with plant, dispatch, logistics, QC, pump, and site stakeholders to keep supply moving.

### C4. Value Selling & Differentiation
Ability to position RDC beyond price:
- reliability
- quality consistency
- service support
- national capability
- digital visibility
- technical support

### C5. Negotiation & Objection Handling
Ability to manage:
- lower competitor prices
- delayed unloading
- quantity disputes
- extra charge objections
- rate mismatch
without losing control or damaging contribution.

### C6. Commercial Acumen & Credit Discipline
Ability to protect price, margin, payment discipline, document clarity, and debit-note exposure.

### C7. Relationship Management & Trust Building
Ability to create repeat business through responsiveness, transparency, follow-up, and issue closure.

### C8. Ownership, Escalation & Follow-through
Ability to take end-to-end ownership:
- not just inform, but drive closure
- know when to escalate
- follow through till resolution
- document learnings

## 6) Randomization and test assembly rules
For each attempt, present **20 questions**.

### Mandatory distribution
Use this target mix:
- C1 Customer Understanding & Need Diagnosis — **2 questions**
- C2 Service Recovery & Complaint Handling — **4 questions**
- C3 Delivery Coordination & Execution Orientation — **3 questions**
- C4 Value Selling & Differentiation — **2 questions**
- C5 Negotiation & Objection Handling — **3 questions**
- C6 Commercial Acumen & Credit Discipline — **3 questions**
- C7 Relationship Management & Trust Building — **1 question**
- C8 Ownership, Escalation & Follow-through — **2 questions**

### Assembly rules
- No ERP questions.
- Avoid near-duplicate questions in the same attempt.
- Avoid asking two almost identical KPI/theory questions together unless needed to fill the pool.
- At least:
  - **6 scenario questions**
  - **6 complaint/disruption questions**
  - **4 commercial/collection questions**
  - **4 business development / relationship questions**

## 7) Voice response rules
- Preferred response length: **30 to 75 seconds**
- Candidate may respond in:
  - English
  - Hinglish
  - Hindi with English RMX terms
- The system must evaluate meaning, not grammar polish.
- Minor hesitation, filler words, and spoken-language fragments should not reduce score unless they make the response unclear.

## 8) Evaluation philosophy
A high-scoring sales response usually shows the following sequence:

1. Recognize the customer impact
2. Verify facts quickly
3. Communicate transparently
4. Offer immediate practical action
5. Coordinate internally
6. Protect company’s commercial position
7. Follow through till closure
8. Reduce recurrence

A weak response usually:
- sounds generic
- jumps to promises without checking facts
- gives unconditional discounts/credit
- blames operations
- ignores documentation
- ignores follow-up
- focuses only on “being polite” instead of solving the problem

## 9) Scoring framework
Each answer is scored on a **1 to 5** scale.

### Sub-scores
#### A. Situational understanding — 20%
Did the candidate understand the real issue, urgency, and customer risk?

#### B. Practical action orientation — 30%
Did the candidate propose actions that can actually be executed in RMX operations?

#### C. Customer communication quality — 20%
Was the response calm, transparent, and confidence-building?

#### D. Commercial judgment — 20%
Did the candidate protect price/margin/payment discipline and avoid casual concessions?

#### E. Ownership & escalation logic — 10%
Did the candidate show clear follow-through and appropriate escalation?

### Score anchors
#### Score 5 — Strong
- Fully relevant to RMX context
- Clear sequence of actions
- Good customer handling
- Good internal coordination
- Commercially balanced
- Strong ownership

#### Score 4 — Good
- Mostly correct
- Practical and customer-focused
- Minor gaps in structure or commercial depth

#### Score 3 — Acceptable
- Basic answer is correct
- Shows intent to solve
- But response is generic, incomplete, or weak on commercial/control aspects

#### Score 2 — Weak
- Misses important actions
- Too vague
- Poor escalation or weak customer handling
- Risk of operational or commercial damage

#### Score 1 — Poor
- Unsafe, unrealistic, careless, or commercially harmful answer
- No ownership
- No RMX understanding

## 10) Mandatory downgrade rules
Cap the score at **3** if any of the following apply:
- answer is generic and not grounded in RMX reality
- candidate says “I will check and revert” without immediate action path
- candidate only apologizes but does not solve
- candidate suggests price reduction / free compensation without verification
- candidate ignores internal coordination

Cap the score at **2** if:
- candidate misleads the customer
- candidate blames plant/QC/logistics in front of the customer
- candidate agrees to improper billing reversal/discount/debit without checking facts
- candidate ignores overdue/payment risk completely

Cap the score at **1** if:
- candidate recommends falsification, concealment, manipulation of documents, or supply continuation against approved stop-supply controls without authorization

## 11) Positive markers
Reward answers that include:
- proactive ETA updates
- realistic alternate plan
- check of pump / site readiness / unloading speed
- document verification before commercial commitment
- complaint logging and closure tracking
- learning loop to avoid recurrence
- protecting trust without surrendering discipline

## 12) Red-flag indicators
Flag for manager review if the candidate repeatedly shows:
- price-first selling
- weak collection discipline
- inability to handle conflict
- excessive dependence on plant/QC for basic customer handling
- poor ownership
- weak prioritization when multiple complaints occur
- tendency to over-promise

## 13) Transcript handling instructions
The evaluator must normalize transcripts before scoring:
- ignore filler words
- normalize common RMX speech variations:
  - “cum” = cubic metre / m3
  - “TM” = transit mixer
  - “pump choke” / “blockage” / “jam”
  - “slump low” / “low slump”
  - “cube fail” / “strength fail”
  - “debit note”
  - “waiting”
- tolerate mixed Hindi-English responses

## 14) Final output format for each question
For each response, produce:
- **Question ID**
- **Competency / competencies**
- **Score (1–5)**
- **Why this score**
- **What was good**
- **What was missing**
- **Manager coaching note**

## 15) Final test summary output
After all 20 questions, produce:
- overall score %
- competency-wise score
- top 3 strengths
- top 3 improvement areas
- red flags, if any
- hiring / development recommendation:
  - Ready
  - Ready with coaching
  - Borderline
  - Not ready

## 16) Practical interpretation guide
### Ready
Can independently handle routine sales/customer-service situations with acceptable judgment.

### Ready with coaching
Can manage most situations but needs structured coaching on negotiation, complaint control, or collections.

### Borderline
Can talk well but lacks practical RMX handling depth or commercial firmness.

### Not ready
Likely to create customer-service or commercial risk without close supervision.

## 17) Source-bank normalization notes
The source file is usable, but the application should apply these clean-up rules:
- remove ERP-only questions (Q85, Q86)
- treat duplicate/similar KPI questions as distinct only if enough variety is required
- merge obvious repeats during randomization logic
- preserve source question text for traceability, but the spoken interface may use lightly polished phrasing

## 18) Gold-standard response pattern
A strong answer typically sounds like:
> “First I acknowledge the customer impact and confirm facts quickly. Then I tell the customer the realistic next step and ETA, while coordinating with plant/logistics/QC internally. I avoid false commitments, protect the company’s commercial position, and stay engaged until closure.”

This pattern should score well when adapted correctly to the specific scenario.

## 19) What this skill file is optimizing for
This test is designed to identify sales people who can:
- sell with discipline
- handle pressure
- keep customer trust during disruptions
- protect margin and collections
- work effectively with RMX operations
